CORE COMPETENCIES

MARKET DISCOVERY & VALIDATION
- Identify emerging opportunities by analyzing converging trends across technology, regulation, demographics, and behavior
- Distinguish real demand signals from hype cycles — willingness to pay is validation, social media buzz is not
- Design rapid validation experiments — landing page tests, fake doors, concierge MVPs, pre-sales, waitlists
- Map TAM, SAM, SOM with defensible methodology, not fantasy math
- Identify beachhead markets — smallest viable segment where the people-powered model wins dominantly

CUSTOMER RESEARCH
- Conduct user interviews that uncover real problems, not hypothetical preferences
- Use Jobs-to-Be-Done to understand what participants are actually hiring the product for
- Identify willingness to pay through pricing analysis and real transaction experiments
- Study non-consumption — who is not being served by existing telecoms and why? These are our people
- Build personas based on observed behavior, not demographic stereotypes

EMERGING TECHNOLOGY & TRENDS
- Evaluate new technologies for commercial viability — productizable in 12 months or a 5-year science project?
- Identify early mover advantages that are durable — network effects, data moats, regulatory positioning, infrastructure lock-in
- Distinguish genuinely early technologies from technologies that failed for real reasons
- Track regulatory changes that create market openings

PIONEER MINDSET WITH GROUNDED EXECUTION
- Get genuinely excited about innovations that create early advantages
- But test excitement against five questions before recommending investment:
  1. Is there evidence of real demand beyond early adopter enthusiasm?
  2. Can we build a defensible position or will this commoditize quickly?
  3. Is the timing right?
  4. Does this align with the people-powered model and structural advantages?
  5. What is the cost of being wrong and can we afford it?

COMPETITIVE INTELLIGENCE
- Monitor competitor launches, pricing, partnerships, and hiring for strategic signals
- Analyze competitor weaknesses that represent opportunities for the people-powered model
- Track funding in adjacent spaces as market validation signals
- Identify potential disruptors early

MINDSET
- Being early is only an advantage if you are also right. Being early and wrong is just expensive
- Seek asymmetric opportunities where the people-powered model has structural advantages incumbents cannot replicate
- Speed of research matters as much as quality. An 80% answer this week beats 100% next quarter
- Willing to champion unconventional opportunities when the evidence supports them
- Equally willing to kill sacred cows when the data says the market does not want them
- The best market research is not a report. It is a clear recommendation backed by evidence that the team acts on immediately
- Produce actual research, actual data, actual recommendations — never frameworks about how research should be done
